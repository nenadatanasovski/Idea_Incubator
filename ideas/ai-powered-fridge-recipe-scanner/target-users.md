# Target User Personas: AI Fridge-to-Meal Recipe App

## Executive Summary

This document profiles the key user segments for the AI-powered fridge scanning recipe app. Understanding these personas is critical for product decisions, marketing strategy, and feature prioritization.

**Total Addressable Market (US):** ~155M potential users
**Serviceable Addressable Market:** ~50M users
**Year 1 Target:** 235K users (including 100K free tier)

---

## Primary Personas

### 1. "Weeknight Warrior" Sarah - The Time-Starved Professional

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Age Range** | 25-45 |
| **Occupation** | White-collar professional (marketing, finance, tech, healthcare) |
| **Household** | Single or DINK (dual income, no kids) |
| **Income** | $60K-150K household |
| **Location** | Urban/suburban apartments or condos |
| **Tech Profile** | High - iPhone/Android power user, 15+ apps daily |

#### Psychographic Profile
- **Values:** Efficiency, work-life balance, self-improvement
- **Lifestyle:** Works 45-60 hours/week, exercises 2-3x/week, socializes on weekends
- **Cooking Relationship:** Wants to cook more but lacks time/energy
- **Decision Style:** Quick decisions, values convenience over perfection

#### User Journey: A Typical Tuesday
| Time | Current State | With App |
|------|---------------|----------|
| 7:00 AM | Rushes out, skips breakfast | Quick scan shows yogurt + berries = smoothie (2 min) |
| 12:30 PM | Buys $15 lunch | Packed lunch from yesterday's suggested recipe |
| 6:30 PM | Opens fridge, decision paralysis | Takes photo, gets 5 options instantly |
| 6:45 PM | Gives up, orders takeout ($28) | Starts 20-min stir-fry recipe |
| 7:30 PM | Eating alone, scrolling phone | Enjoying homemade dinner, saved $25 |

#### Pain Points (Ranked by Severity)
1. **Decision fatigue** - Too tired to think about what to cook (9/10)
2. **Food waste guilt** - Watches vegetables rot every week (8/10)
3. **Health concerns** - Knows takeout habit is unhealthy (7/10)
4. **Money waste** - Spends $200+/month on unnecessary takeout (6/10)
5. **Recipe overwhelm** - Can't find recipes for specific ingredients (6/10)

#### Jobs to Be Done
| Job | Importance | Current Solution | Our Advantage |
|-----|------------|------------------|---------------|
| Get dinner on table in <30 min | High | Takeout, meal kits | Instant, personalized |
| Use ingredients before they expire | High | Mental notes (fails) | Visual AI tracking |
| Eat healthier without planning | Medium | Sporadic attempts | Effortless suggestions |
| Reduce food waste | Medium | None | Expiration alerts |

#### Willingness to Pay
- **Price Sensitivity:** Medium
- **Comparable Spending:** Netflix ($15), Gym ($40), Coffee ($100)
- **Target Price:** $7.99-12.99/month
- **Key Value Trigger:** Time saved Ã— guilt reduced
- **Churn Risk:** Medium - needs consistent value demonstration

#### Feature Priorities
1. One-tap fridge scanning (essential)
2. Quick recipes (<30 min filter)
3. Smart expiration alerts
4. Voice-guided cooking
5. Shopping list generation

#### Sample Quote
> "I actually have food in my fridge - I just can't figure out what to do with it."

### 2. "Macro Mike" - The Fitness-Focused Tracker

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Age Range** | 22-38 |
| **Occupation** | Knowledge worker (tech, consulting, finance) |
| **Fitness Level** | Gym 4-6x/week, tracks everything |
| **Diet** | High protein, macro counting (IIFYM, RP, Renaissance) |
| **Income** | $70K-140K |
| **Tech Profile** | Uses MyFitnessPal, fitness trackers, smart scale |

#### Psychographic Profile
- **Values:** Optimization, discipline, results, data
- **Lifestyle:** Meal prep Sunday, gym before work, protein at every meal
- **Cooking Relationship:** Functional - fuel not pleasure
- **Decision Style:** Data-driven, will research extensively

#### User Journey: Meal Prep Sunday
| Time | Current State | With App |
|------|---------------|----------|
| 9:00 AM | Plans 5 meals manually | Scans fridge, gets 20 high-protein options |
| 9:30 AM | Calculates macros for each meal | Auto-calculated, sortable by protein |
| 10:00 AM | Writes shopping list | Missing ingredients auto-added |
| 11:00 AM | Shops, forgets items | List synced to phone |
| 2:00 PM | Batch cooking 3 boring meals | 5 varied meals, same macros |

#### Pain Points (Ranked by Severity)
1. **Meal boredom** - Same 5 recipes on rotation (9/10)
2. **Macro calculation** - Tedious for homemade meals (8/10)
3. **Protein variety** - Chicken fatigue is real (8/10)
4. **Time efficiency** - Wants max nutrition, min effort (7/10)
5. **Recipe adaptation** - Can't easily modify recipes for macros (6/10)

#### Jobs to Be Done
| Job | Importance | Current Solution | Our Advantage |
|-----|------------|------------------|---------------|
| Hit macro targets daily | Critical | Manual tracking | Auto-calculation |
| Find high-protein recipes | High | Reddit, YouTube | Instant, personalized |
| Keep meals interesting | High | None (suffers) | AI variety engine |
| Efficient meal prep | Medium | Self-optimized | Batch-ready recipes |

#### Willingness to Pay
- **Price Sensitivity:** Low for fitness-related
- **Comparable Spending:** Gym ($50), Supplements ($80), MFP Premium ($20)
- **Target Price:** $12.99-19.99/month
- **Key Value Trigger:** Macro accuracy + meal variety
- **Churn Risk:** Low if macros are accurate, high if not

#### Feature Priorities
1. Accurate macro calculation per serving
2. Protein/carb/fat sorting and filtering
3. MyFitnessPal integration
4. Batch cooking/meal prep mode
5. Recipe scaling by macro targets

#### Sample Quote
> "If I have to eat another plain chicken breast, I'm going to scream."

### 3. "Budget Betty" - The Family Meal Planner

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Age Range** | 30-50 |
| **Role** | Primary household meal planner (often mom) |
| **Household** | 3-6 people, typically 2 parents + 1-4 kids |
| **Income** | $50K-100K household |
| **Location** | Suburban homes |
| **Shopping Style** | Weekly grocery run, uses coupons/apps |

#### Psychographic Profile
- **Values:** Family health, financial responsibility, tradition
- **Lifestyle:** Busy managing household, kids' activities, possibly working
- **Cooking Relationship:** Daily necessity, wants it to be enjoyable
- **Decision Style:** Practical, considers whole family

#### User Journey: Weeknight Dinner
| Time | Current State | With App |
|------|---------------|----------|
| 4:00 PM | Realizes no dinner plan | Gets notification: "Chicken expires tomorrow - 8 recipes" |
| 4:15 PM | Kids complain about suggestions | Filters for "kid-friendly" |
| 5:00 PM | Missing ingredient, can't make recipe | App shows substitutions |
| 5:30 PM | Cooking while answering homework questions | Voice-guided instructions, hands-free |
| 6:30 PM | One kid won't eat it | Knew ahead - made backup portion |

#### Pain Points (Ranked by Severity)
1. **Budget pressure** - Grocery costs keep rising (9/10)
2. **Picky eaters** - Finding meals everyone will eat (9/10)
3. **Food waste** - Can't afford to throw food away (8/10)
4. **Time crunch** - Must cook after work, before activities (7/10)
5. **Variety fatigue** - Same 10 recipes on rotation (6/10)

#### Jobs to Be Done
| Job | Importance | Current Solution | Our Advantage |
|-----|------------|------------------|---------------|
| Feed family within budget | Critical | Sales circulars, planning | Waste reduction |
| Find meals everyone eats | High | Trial and error | Family preference profiles |
| Reduce food waste | High | Tries to use everything | Smart expiration alerts |
| Weekly meal planning | Medium | Spreadsheets, paper | Auto-generated plans |

#### Willingness to Pay
- **Price Sensitivity:** High - every dollar matters
- **Comparable Spending:** Streaming ($15), Costco ($60/year)
- **Target Price:** $4.99-7.99/month (or annual discount)
- **Key Value Trigger:** Demonstrable savings (>$30/month saved)
- **Churn Risk:** Medium - must show clear ROI

#### Feature Priorities
1. Budget-friendly recipe filtering
2. Family/kid-friendly tags
3. Ingredient substitution suggestions
4. Batch cooking and leftover transformation
5. Weekly meal planning calendar
6. Serving size scaling

#### Sample Quote
> "Between groceries and kid activities, I can't afford to throw food away. But I also can't serve the same 5 meals forever."

### 4. "First-Timer Finn" - The Kitchen Novice

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Age Range** | 18-26 |
| **Life Stage** | College student or recent graduate |
| **Living Situation** | Dorm, apartment with roommates, first solo apartment |
| **Income** | $0-40K (part-time work, entry-level job) |
| **Kitchen Setup** | Minimal - microwave, one pan, basic utensils |
| **Tech Profile** | Native - TikTok, Instagram, YouTube for everything |

#### Psychographic Profile
- **Values:** Independence, social approval, authenticity
- **Lifestyle:** Classes/early career, social life priority, irregular schedule
- **Cooking Relationship:** Intimidated but curious
- **Decision Style:** Influenced by peers and social media

#### User Journey: Saturday Experiment
| Time | Current State | With App |
|------|---------------|----------|
| 12:00 PM | Wakes up, fridge has random items | Scans: eggs, cheese, hot sauce, bread |
| 12:05 PM | Google: "what to make with eggs" - overwhelming | App: "5 recipes with your ingredients" |
| 12:10 PM | Picks complex recipe, missing 8 items | Picks "Cheesy Egg Sandwich" - 4 ingredients |
| 12:15 PM | Doesn't know cooking terms | Step-by-step with video clips |
| 12:30 PM | Burns it, eats cereal | Makes decent sandwich, shares on IG |

#### Pain Points (Ranked by Severity)
1. **Knowledge gap** - Doesn't know basic techniques (9/10)
2. **Limited budget** - Can't buy specialty ingredients (8/10)
3. **Recipe overwhelm** - All recipes assume you can cook (8/10)
4. **Small portions** - Recipes make too much food (7/10)
5. **Limited equipment** - No blender, mixer, etc. (6/10)

#### Jobs to Be Done
| Job | Importance | Current Solution | Our Advantage |
|-----|------------|------------------|---------------|
| Make edible food cheaply | Critical | Ramen, fast food | Simple recipes |
| Learn cooking basics | High | YouTube rabbit holes | Integrated tutorials |
| Impress friends/dates | Medium | Order in | "Fancy" easy recipes |
| Not waste food | Medium | Doesn't buy much | Helps use random items |

#### Willingness to Pay
- **Price Sensitivity:** Very high - everything is a splurge
- **Comparable Spending:** Spotify ($5 student), Netflix (shared)
- **Target Price:** Free tier essential, $2.99-4.99 premium
- **Key Value Trigger:** Actually succeeding at cooking
- **Churn Risk:** High if free, low if converted

#### Feature Priorities
1. Beginner-friendly recipes with technique explanations
2. Minimal ingredient requirements (<5 ingredients)
3. Equipment filtering (no-oven, one-pan)
4. Video/GIF step-by-step instructions
5. Single-serving portions
6. Social sharing features

#### Sample Quote
> "I bought groceries but I literally don't know how to combine them into food."

### 5. "Retired Rita" - The Empty Nester

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Age Range** | 55-75 |
| **Life Stage** | Retired or semi-retired, kids moved out |
| **Household** | 1-2 people |
| **Income** | Fixed income, comfortable |
| **Health Focus** | Heart health, diabetes prevention, weight management |
| **Tech Profile** | Moderate - uses tablet, smartphone, some apps |

#### Psychographic Profile
- **Values:** Health, quality over quantity, tradition, trying new things
- **Lifestyle:** More time, less energy; travels; grandkid duties
- **Cooking Relationship:** Decades of experience, in a rut
- **Decision Style:** Thoughtful, research-oriented

#### User Journey: Tuesday Dinner for Two
| Time | Current State | With App |
|------|---------------|----------|
| 4:00 PM | Opens fridge, same ingredients as always | Scans, discovers new recipe for familiar items |
| 4:10 PM | Family recipes serve 6+ | Filters: "2 servings" |
| 4:15 PM | Worries about sodium | Heart-healthy filter applied |
| 4:30 PM | Starts cooking with new recipe | Clear instructions, larger text option |
| 6:00 PM | Surprised partner with something new | Both enjoyed, will make again |

#### Pain Points (Ranked by Severity)
1. **Portion sizing** - Everything is sized for families (8/10)
2. **Recipe boredom** - Cooking same things for 30+ years (8/10)
3. **Health constraints** - Low sodium, diabetic-friendly needs (7/10)
4. **Food waste** - Can't eat large quantities before spoilage (7/10)
5. **Cooking motivation** - Less fun cooking for 2 (6/10)

#### Jobs to Be Done
| Job | Importance | Current Solution | Our Advantage |
|-----|------------|------------------|---------------|
| Find recipes for 2 people | High | Halving math | Auto-scaling |
| Cook within health limits | High | Trusted cookbooks | Filtered recipes |
| Reduce monotony | Medium | Occasional new cookbook | Endless variety |
| Not waste food | Medium | Smaller quantities | Use-it-up suggestions |

#### Willingness to Pay
- **Price Sensitivity:** Low-medium - value quality
- **Comparable Spending:** Cookbooks ($25), Classes ($100+)
- **Target Price:** $7.99-11.99/month
- **Key Value Trigger:** Discovery of new favorites
- **Churn Risk:** Low once habits form

#### Feature Priorities
1. Portion size adjustments (1-2 servings)
2. Health condition filters (heart, diabetic, etc.)
3. Larger text and clear interface
4. Print-friendly recipes
5. Familiar cuisine with new twists

#### Sample Quote
> "I've been cooking for 40 years and I'm bored of my own cooking."

## Appendix: Research Methods

This persona document should be validated through:

1. **Quantitative Survey** (n=500+)
   - Demographics and cooking habits
   - Pain point severity ranking
   - Willingness to pay testing
   - Feature priority ranking

2. **Qualitative Interviews** (n=20-30)
   - Day-in-the-life walkthroughs
   - Kitchen observation sessions
   - Current tool/app usage
   - Purchase decision factors

3. **Competitive User Research**
   - Interview users of Supercook, Yummly, Whisk
   - App store review analysis
   - Subreddit sentiment analysis

4. **Beta Testing**
   - Recruit 100 users across personas
   - Track feature usage by segment
   - Measure retention and engagement
   - Gather qualitative feedback
