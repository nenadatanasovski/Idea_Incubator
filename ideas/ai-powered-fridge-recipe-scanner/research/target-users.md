# Target Users: AI-Powered Recipe App

## Executive Summary

This document identifies and profiles the primary, secondary, and tertiary target user segments for an AI-powered recipe app that scans fridge contents and suggests recipes. Understanding these users is critical for product design, marketing strategy, and feature prioritization.

---

## Primary Target Users

### 1. Busy Professionals (Ages 25-45)

**Profile:**
- Working 40-60+ hours per week
- Limited time for meal planning and grocery shopping
- Often resort to takeout or meal delivery due to time constraints
- Tech-savvy and comfortable with mobile apps

**Pain Points:**
- Food waste from forgotten ingredients
- Decision fatigue after long workdays ("What should I cook?")
- Guilt about unhealthy eating habits
- Wasted time browsing recipe sites without knowing what's available

**Jobs to Be Done:**
- Quickly decide what to make with available ingredients
- Reduce mental load of meal planning
- Minimize grocery trips and food waste
- Eat healthier without significant time investment

**Willingness to Pay:** High ($8-15/month)

**Key Features Valued:**
- Quick recipe suggestions (under 30 min cook time)
- One-tap fridge scanning
- Meal prep and batch cooking options
- Integration with grocery delivery services

---

### 2. Health-Conscious Home Cooks (Ages 28-55)

**Profile:**
- Actively tracking nutrition, macros, or following specific diets
- Regular home cooking (4-7 times per week)
- Interested in ingredient quality and sourcing
- May have dietary restrictions (keto, vegan, gluten-free, etc.)

**Pain Points:**
- Finding recipes that fit specific nutritional requirements
- Calculating nutritional info for homemade meals
- Ingredient substitutions that maintain diet compliance
- Boredom with limited diet-compliant recipes

**Jobs to Be Done:**
- Find recipes matching dietary restrictions
- Track nutritional intake accurately
- Discover new compliant recipes
- Substitute ingredients without breaking diet rules

**Willingness to Pay:** High ($10-20/month for premium nutrition features)

**Key Features Valued:**
- Dietary filter presets (keto, paleo, vegan, etc.)
- Nutritional breakdown per serving
- Smart substitution suggestions
- Integration with fitness/health apps (MyFitnessPal, Apple Health)

---

### 3. Budget-Conscious Families (Ages 30-50)

**Profile:**
- Households with children
- Managing tight food budgets
- Cooking most meals at home
- Concerned about food waste and stretching ingredients

**Pain Points:**
- High grocery bills
- Picky eaters limiting recipe options
- Food spoiling before use
- Planning meals that satisfy everyone

**Jobs to Be Done:**
- Maximize value from purchased groceries
- Reduce food waste significantly
- Find kid-friendly recipes
- Plan weekly meals efficiently

**Willingness to Pay:** Medium ($5-10/month, very price-sensitive)

**Key Features Valued:**
- Cost-per-serving estimates
- Kid-friendly recipe filters
- "Use it before it expires" alerts
- Batch cooking and freezer meal suggestions
- Weekly meal planning with shopping lists

---

## Secondary Target Users

### 4. College Students & Young Adults (Ages 18-25)

**Profile:**
- Limited cooking experience
- Small budgets and minimal kitchen equipment
- Living in dorms or small apartments
- Learning to cook for the first time

**Pain Points:**
- Don't know how to cook basic meals
- Limited ingredients and equipment
- Recipes feel intimidating
- Wasting money on unused groceries

**Jobs to Be Done:**
- Learn cooking basics with guidance
- Make simple, cheap meals
- Build confidence in the kitchen
- Avoid embarrassing cooking failures

**Willingness to Pay:** Low (freemium users, ad-supported)

**Key Features Valued:**
- Beginner-friendly recipes with video guidance
- Equipment filters (no oven, microwave-only, etc.)
- Step-by-step instructions with timers
- Very simple ingredient lists

---

### 5. Empty Nesters & Retirees (Ages 55-75)

**Profile:**
- More time available for cooking
- Cooking for 1-2 people (portion challenges)
- May have health conditions requiring dietary changes
- Less tech-savvy but willing to learn

**Pain Points:**
- Recipes designed for 4+ servings
- Managing new dietary restrictions (low sodium, diabetic-friendly)
- Loneliness of cooking for one
- Adapting lifetime recipes to health needs

**Jobs to Be Done:**
- Scale recipes to smaller portions
- Find heart-healthy, diabetic-friendly options
- Reduce food waste from large recipes
- Maintain cooking as enjoyable activity

**Willingness to Pay:** Medium-High ($8-12/month)

**Key Features Valued:**
- Portion scaling (cook for 1-2)
- Health condition filters
- Large, readable text and simple UI
- Voice-guided cooking mode

---

### 6. Food Enthusiasts & Hobbyist Cooks (Ages 25-55)

**Profile:**
- Cooking as a hobby/passion
- Interested in trying new cuisines and techniques
- Active on food social media
- Willing to spend on quality ingredients

**Pain Points:**
- Recipe boredom and repetition
- Finding inspiration for available ingredients
- Organizing personal recipe collections
- Discovering restaurant-quality techniques

**Jobs to Be Done:**
- Get creative inspiration from what's available
- Learn new cuisines and techniques
- Impress family/friends with dishes
- Document and organize personal creations

**Willingness to Pay:** High ($15-25/month for premium features)

**Key Features Valued:**
- Cuisine exploration and world recipes
- Advanced technique tutorials
- Recipe saving and personal collections
- Social sharing and community features
- Chef tips and pro techniques

---

## Tertiary/Niche Target Users

### 7. People with Allergies/Intolerances

**Specific Needs:**
- Allergen detection in ingredient lists
- Safe substitution recommendations
- Cross-contamination warnings
- Verified allergen-free recipes

### 8. Meal Preppers & Fitness Enthusiasts

**Specific Needs:**
- Bulk cooking calculations
- Macro-optimized recipes
- Storage and reheating instructions
- Protein-forward recipe options

### 9. Sustainability-Focused Users

**Specific Needs:**
- Zero-waste recipe suggestions
- Seasonal/local ingredient emphasis
- Carbon footprint estimates
- Plant-forward cooking options

### 10. Caregivers & Parents of Picky Eaters

**Specific Needs:**
- Hidden vegetable recipes
- Texture/sensory-friendly options
- Allergy-safe school lunch ideas
- Gradual exposure suggestions

## Key Insights for Product Development

### Must-Have Features (All Primary Users):
1. **Fast, accurate fridge scanning** - The core value proposition
2. **Smart filtering** - Time, difficulty, dietary restrictions
3. **Step-by-step guidance** - With timers and photos
4. **Shopping list generation** - For missing ingredients
5. **Recipe saving/favorites** - Personal collections

### Differentiating Features:
1. **Expiration tracking** - "Use these first" prioritization
2. **Cost estimation** - Per-serving cost calculations
3. **Nutritional integration** - Sync with health apps
4. **Portion scaling** - Cook for 1 or 10
5. **Voice mode** - Hands-free cooking guidance

### Monetization Implications:
- **Freemium base:** Basic scanning + limited recipes/day
- **Premium tier ($9.99/month):** Unlimited recipes, nutrition tracking, advanced filters
- **Family plan ($14.99/month):** Multiple profiles, shared lists, kid-friendly features
- **Pro tier ($19.99/month):** Meal planning, grocery delivery integration, exclusive content
