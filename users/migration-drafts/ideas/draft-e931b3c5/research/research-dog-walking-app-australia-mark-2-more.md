## Key Findings

1. **Global market valued at ~$500-600M in 2024** - The dog walking app market globally ranges from USD 508-600 million in 2024, with projections to reach $1-1.2 billion by 2033 (CAGR of 8-9%)

2. **Australia-specific pet care app market is small but growing** - The Australian pet care app market (including dog walking) is estimated at USD 24.10 million in 2024, growing at a strong CAGR of 19.7%

3. **Asia-Pacific is the fastest growing region** - Australia sits in the APAC region which holds 20% market share and is experiencing the highest growth, driven by increasing pet adoption and mobile app usage

4. **Government support exists** - Australian pet service providers have received government grants ($18 million noted) to integrate tech innovation including real-time vet support features

5. **North America dominates (40% share)** - The market is still heavily concentrated in North America and Europe, suggesting Australia is relatively underserved

## Key Findings

1. **Mad Paws acquired by Rover (Nov 2025)** - Mad Paws was acquired by Rover Group at a $40.38M valuation, consolidating market leadership. Mad Paws was Australia's largest pet care marketplace with 50,000+ service providers and 2M+ completed bookings.

2. **Duopoly market structure** - The Australian market is primarily dominated by Mad Paws and Pawshake. With Mad Paws now owned by Rover (the global leader), the competitive landscape has shifted significantly.

3. **Different business models** - Mad Paws takes a 20% commission on bookings; Pawshake has no subscription or booking fees for pet care (likely monetizes differently). Mad Paws also operates a pet pharmacy, diversifying revenue.

4. **Australia ranks #3 globally** - With 11 pet sitting marketplace companies, Australia is the third-largest market after the US (54 companies) and India (26).

5. **Established partnerships matter** - Mad Paws partnered with Qantas Frequent Flyer, offering loyalty points on bookings - a significant competitive advantage for customer retention.

## Pet Services Marketplace Australia - Research Summary

### Key Findings

1. **Massive and growing market**: Australia's pet industry is worth $33 billion, with pet sitting services alone at $1.5 billion (9% CAGR). Pet care products expected to nearly double from $6.59B to $12.73B by 2033.

2. **Surging pet ownership**: Over two-thirds of Australian households now own pets (up from 61% in 2019), with 800,000+ new pet-owning households and 2.9 million more animals in just three years.

3. **Digital shift accelerating**: 35% of pet owners now purchase online (up from 20% in 2019). App-based and in-home services are preferred over traditional boarding. Smart/AI pet tech is emerging.

4. **Highly fragmented market**: No company holds >5% market share in pet sitting. There are 5,705 pet sitting businesses (12.4% CAGR in business growth 2020-2025) - indicating opportunity for consolidation/platform play.

5. **Pet humanization trend**: Owners spend ~$3,300/dog and $2,100/cat annually, treating pets as family members. This drives demand for premium, personalized services.

### Concerns / Red Flags

- **Cost-of-living crisis**: Economic pressures may cause pet owners to cut back on premium services
- **Low barriers to entry**: 5,700+ businesses indicates easy market entry but also intense competition
- **Trust challenges**: Pet sitting is intimate - building trust at scale is difficult
- **Fragmentation may be intentional**: Highly localized, personal nature of service may resist platformization

### Opportunities Identified

1. **Consolidation platform**: Fragmented market with no dominant player - opportunity for a marketplace to aggregate supply
2. **Premium/trust-focused positioning**: Pet humanization trend supports premium pricing for vetted, high-quality services
3. **Technology differentiation**: Smart pet tech integration (GPS, cameras, health monitoring) can differentiate
4. **Bundled services**: Combine sitting, walking, grooming, and pet supplies for higher LTV
5. **Subscription models**: Regular dog walking/daycare services suit recurring revenue models
