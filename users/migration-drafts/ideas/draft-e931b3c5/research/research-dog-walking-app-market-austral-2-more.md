## Dog Walking App Market Australia 2024 - Research Summary

### Key Findings

- **Global market growth**: The dog walking app market is projected to grow from ~$509M (2024) to ~$1.08B by 2033 at 8.67% CAGR, indicating strong long-term growth potential
- **High urban adoption**: 62% of dog owners in urban areas use mobile-based pet care services, with over 120 million walks booked globally in 2023
- **Australia-specific players exist**: PetCloud (100% Australian-owned) and Pawshake are already operating in the Australian market alongside global giants like Rover and Wag!
- **Government support**: Australian pet service providers received $18M in government grants to promote local tech innovation, including integration with real-time vet support
- **Asia Pacific opportunity**: While North America leads (40% market share), Asia Pacific is the fastest-growing region driven by increasing pet adoption

## Pet Care Marketplace Research: Australia

### Key Findings

1. **Market dominated by established players** - The Australian pet retail market (~$4.1B in 2025) is controlled by Greencross Group (largest market share), PETstock, and the rapidly growing PetO (tripled revenue to A$200M). The ACCC has raised concerns about potential duopoly formation.

2. **Mad Paws is the dominant pet sitting marketplace** - Sydney-based Mad Paws operates an online marketplace connecting pet owners with local sitters, charging a 15% commission. Australia has only 11 pet sitting marketplace companies, ranking 3rd globally behind USA (54) and India (26).

3. **Major retailers expanding into digital marketplaces** - Coles launched Swaggle as a dedicated pet marketplace, recognizing pets need specialized curation. Petbarn (14.67%), Petcircle (13.7%), and Amazon (5.37%) compete heavily in online PPC advertising.

4. **Pet food market growing steadily** - Worth $3.91B in 2025, projected to reach $4.99B by 2030 (5.03% CAGR), dominated by global players (Mars, Nestle Purina, Hill's).

5. **Emerging integrated platforms** - PetNotch offers comprehensive services (grooming, pet taxi, boarding) plus community features (lost & found, play dates, buy/sell marketplace), indicating demand for all-in-one solutions.

### Concerns & Red Flags

- **Market consolidation risk** - ACCC has flagged duopoly concerns; entering against Greencross/PETstock's scale is difficult
- **Mad Paws is well-established** - Already has brand recognition and network effects in pet sitting space
- **Low startup density** - Only 11 pet sitting platforms in Australia suggests either: (a) limited market opportunity, or (b) high barriers to entry/winner-take-all dynamics
- **Major retailers moving in** - Coles (Swaggle), Petbarn, and Petstock have resources to dominate digital channels
- **Global giants in pet food** - Mars, Nestle, Colgate-Palmolive control supply chain

### Opportunities Identified

- **Underserved niche services** - PetNotch's success with grooming, pet taxi, and community features suggests demand beyond basic sitting
- **Regional/local focus** - National players may underserve specific metro or regional areas
- **Trust & vetting differentiation** - Opportunity to differentiate on sitter quality, insurance, or specialized care (elderly pets, medical needs)
- **B2B services** - Pet-friendly workplaces, corporate pet care benefits largely untapped
- **Integration with vet services** - Connecting care marketplace with veterinary/health services could create unique value proposition
